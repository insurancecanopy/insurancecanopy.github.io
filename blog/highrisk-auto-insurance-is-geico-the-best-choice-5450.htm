High-Risk Auto Insurance: Is GEICO the Best Choice?

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

Navigating the world of high-risk auto insurance can feel like driving through a storm without headlights. Whether you’ve had multiple accidents, a DUI, or a lapse in coverage, finding affordable and reliable coverage is a challenge. Among the sea of insurers, GEICO often stands out—but is it truly the best choice for high-risk drivers? Let’s break it down.

What Makes a Driver "High-Risk"?

Insurance companies categorize drivers based on risk factors that make them more likely to file claims. Some common reasons include:

1. Poor Driving Record

Multiple speeding tickets, at-fault accidents, or reckless driving violations can label you as high-risk.

2. DUI or DWI Convictions

A single DUI can skyrocket your premiums and limit your insurance options.

3. Young or Inexperienced Drivers

Teens and new drivers often face higher rates due to lack of experience.

4. Lapses in Coverage

Going without insurance, even for a short period, raises red flags for insurers.

5. Low Credit Scores

In many states, insurers use credit-based insurance scores to determine risk.

How GEICO Handles High-Risk Drivers

GEICO is known for competitive rates and a user-friendly experience, but how does it stack up for high-risk drivers?

GEICO’s High-Risk Insurance Options

GEICO doesn’t have a dedicated "high-risk" division, but it does offer standard policies that may accommodate drivers with blemished records. However, eligibility varies by state, and some high-risk drivers may be referred to GEICO’s affiliate, The General, which specializes in non-standard (high-risk) insurance.

Pros of Choosing GEICO

  • Competitive Pricing: GEICO often beats competitors on price, even for higher-risk drivers.
  • Strong Financial Stability: GEICO’s A++ (Superior) rating from AM Best means it can reliably pay claims.
  • Discount Opportunities: Safe driver discounts, multi-policy savings, and military discounts can help offset high premiums.

Cons of Choosing GEICO

  • Not Always Available: Some high-risk drivers may be denied coverage and directed to The General instead.
  • Limited High-Risk Specialization: Unlike companies like Progressive or Dairyland, GEICO doesn’t focus exclusively on high-risk policies.

Alternatives to GEICO for High-Risk Drivers

If GEICO isn’t the right fit, here are some other top insurers to consider:

1. Progressive

  • Specializes in high-risk drivers.
  • Offers SR-22 and FR-44 filings (often required after DUIs).
  • Usage-based Snapshot program can help lower rates over time.

2. State Farm

  • More lenient with minor violations.
  • Local agents provide personalized service.

3. The General

  • GEICO’s affiliate for non-standard insurance.
  • Accepts drivers with DUIs, suspensions, and no prior coverage.

4. Dairyland

  • Known for covering extreme high-risk cases.
  • Higher premiums but more flexible underwriting.

How to Lower Your High-Risk Insurance Rates

Even if you’re stuck with high premiums now, there are ways to reduce costs over time:

1. Take a Defensive Driving Course

Many insurers offer discounts for completing an approved course.

2. Maintain Continuous Coverage

Avoid lapses to show insurers you’re responsible.

3. Improve Your Credit Score

In states where it’s allowed, a better credit score can lower premiums.

4. Drive Safely

Avoid tickets and accidents to gradually improve your risk profile.

5. Shop Around Annually

Rates change, and loyalty doesn’t always pay—compare quotes every year.

Final Thoughts: Is GEICO the Best for High-Risk Drivers?

GEICO can be a solid option for some high-risk drivers, especially those with minor infractions or those who qualify for discounts. However, if your record includes serious violations like a DUI, you might need a more specialized insurer like Progressive or The General.

The key is to shop around, compare quotes, and focus on improving your driving habits to eventually transition back to standard insurance. High-risk status isn’t permanent—with time and responsible driving, you can regain control of your premiums.
