Is Rideshare Insurance Expensive? How to Lower Costs

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

Rideshare driving has become a popular side hustle or full-time career for millions of people worldwide. With companies like Uber, Lyft, and Didi dominating the market, more drivers are hitting the road to earn extra income. However, one critical aspect often overlooked is rideshare insurance—how much it costs and how to reduce those expenses.

Understanding Rideshare Insurance

Rideshare insurance is a specialized policy designed to fill the gaps between personal auto insurance and commercial coverage. Standard personal auto insurance typically doesn’t cover accidents that occur while you’re logged into a rideshare app, even if you don’t have a passenger.

Why Do You Need Rideshare Insurance?

Most rideshare companies provide some level of insurance, but it’s often limited:

  • Period 1 (App Off): No coverage—personal insurance applies.
  • Period 2 (App On, No Ride): Limited liability coverage from the rideshare company.
  • Period 3 (Passenger in Car): Higher coverage from the rideshare company.

However, gaps remain, especially in Period 2, where your personal insurer might deny a claim if they discover you were driving for a rideshare platform.

How Much Does Rideshare Insurance Cost?

Rideshare insurance isn’t necessarily expensive, but costs vary based on several factors:

Factors Affecting Rideshare Insurance Premiums

  1. Location – Urban areas with higher accident rates may have pricier premiums.
  2. Driving History – A clean record means lower costs.
  3. Vehicle Type – Newer or luxury cars cost more to insure.
  4. Coverage Limits – Higher liability limits increase premiums.
  5. Insurance Provider – Some companies specialize in rideshare policies with competitive rates.

On average, rideshare insurance adds $15–$30 per month to a standard auto policy. While this isn’t exorbitant, every dollar counts when you’re trying to maximize earnings.

How to Lower Your Rideshare Insurance Costs

If you’re looking to cut expenses without sacrificing coverage, here are some proven strategies:

1. Compare Multiple Insurance Providers

Not all insurers offer rideshare endorsements, and pricing varies. Companies like State Farm, Allstate, and Progressive provide rideshare-friendly policies. Use comparison tools to find the best deal.

2. Bundle Policies for Discounts

If you already have renters or homeowners insurance with a provider, bundling your rideshare coverage could save you 10–20%.

3. Maintain a Clean Driving Record

Traffic violations and accidents spike premiums. Defensive driving courses can sometimes offset minor infractions.

4. Opt for a Higher Deductible

Choosing a $1,000 deductible instead of $500 can lower monthly payments—just ensure you have savings to cover the difference if needed.

5. Drive a Less Expensive Vehicle

Insuring a Toyota Corolla is cheaper than a Tesla. If possible, use a budget-friendly car for rideshare driving.

6. Limit Mileage

Some insurers offer discounts for low-mileage drivers. If you only drive part-time, ask about pay-per-mile policies.

7. Take Advantage of Rideshare Company Perks

Uber and Lyft sometimes partner with insurers to offer discounted rates—check their driver portals for promotions.

The Future of Rideshare Insurance

With the rise of autonomous vehicles and EV fleets, insurance models may shift. Some experts predict usage-based insurance (UBI) will become standard, where premiums adjust based on real-time driving behavior.

Additionally, as gig economy regulations evolve, lawmakers may push for more standardized (and potentially subsidized) insurance options for rideshare drivers.

Final Thoughts

Rideshare insurance doesn’t have to break the bank. By shopping around, leveraging discounts, and maintaining safe driving habits, you can secure affordable coverage while protecting yourself on the road. Whether you drive occasionally or full-time, the right insurance strategy ensures you keep more of your hard-earned money.
